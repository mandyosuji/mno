Hello, I'm Mandy Osuji.

My creative work often circles the theme of memory and its lifecycle. I enjoy different forms and have done projects across mediums, including fabric, the web, writing, and sound. This is a home for said projects.

01 — Identity

Who are you at eighteen?

At eighteen, identity is still forming — shaped by friendships, family expectations, the neighborhoods you grew up in, and the futures you've started to imagine for yourself.

02 — Body

Learning to live in your own skin.

The body at eighteen carries the marks of childhood and the anxiety of adulthood. It is scrutinized, celebrated, misunderstood, and slowly, tentatively, owned.

03 — Family

The pull of where you come from.

Home is simultaneously the place you want to escape and the anchor you'll spend years understanding. At eighteen, that tension is at its most acute.

04 — School

What it means to keep learning.

High school ends. Something else begins. The classroom expands — or disappears entirely — and the question of what education is for becomes suddenly very real.

05 — Future

Staring down the years ahead.

The future at eighteen is vast and terrifying and full of possibility. Plans feel both urgent and arbitrary. Everything seems to hinge on choices you're not sure you're ready to make.

06 — World

Arriving just as everything shifts.

Each generation inherits the world at a particular moment in history. To be eighteen now is to grow up inside a specific set of anxieties, hopes, and contradictions that are entirely your own.
